Students from Goldenstate College of Koronadal, alongside faculty Sir AJ Bryant Jalando‑on and Sir Reyn Heart Floro, took part in the “Development of City of Koronadal – Creative Industry Development Plan (CK‑CIDP) Workshop.” The event took place at Paraiso Verde Resort and Waterpark from December 4 to 6 as part of the city’s Koronadaleño Lunsod Lunsad 2024 initiative.

Organized by the City Tourism and Cultural Affairs Office, the free workshop brought together digital creatives, artists, local enterprises, and institutions with sessions focused on digital content creation and marketing strategies.

Over the course of the three-day event, participants engaged in hands-on activities aimed at enhancing their skills in crafting compelling digital content and strengthening online marketing techniques. The workshop was described as both “purposeful and fun-filled,” offering practical insights meant to elevate participants’ creative and promotional capabilities.

The CK‑CIDP workshop is part of a broader push under DTI’s Lunsod Lunsad program, which supports local creative industries by providing funding, technical assistance, and capacity-building workshops across municipalities—including Koronadal, a recognized recipient of a P2‑million grant for creative industry development.
